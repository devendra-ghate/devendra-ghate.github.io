Gas Constant

The universal gas constant for an ideal gas is \(R_u = 8.3145\) J/mol/K and

\[pV = nR_uT = (m/M)R_uT\]

where, \(n\) is the number of moles of the gas. Also, \(m\) is the mass of the gas and \(M\) is the molar mass of the gas.

This gives us

\[pv = p (V/m) = (R_u/M)T = RT\]

where \(R = R_u/M\) is the gas specific constant. This is a mass specific constant.

On the other hand,

\[ p\hat{v} = p(V/n) = R_uT = \hat{R} T \]

where \(\hat{v}\) is the mole specific volume and \(\hat{R} = R_u\) is the mole specific gas constant.

In the question 11, the ratio \(R/\hat{R} = (R_u/M)/R_u = 1/M\).
