Review of Thermodynamics

Q: An adiabatic gas chamber has a diaphragm separating the chamber in two halves. The pressure difference between the two sides is large enough to cause shock waves. The gas achieves a steady state after some time. Can we apply the first law of thermodynamics during this problem? If yes, under what assumptions?

Q: For a perfect gas, show that

\[\begin{aligned} \displaystyle c_v & = \frac{\partial e}{\partial T}, \ \ \text{and}\ \ c_p = \frac{\partial e}{\partial T} + \left( \frac{\partial e}{\partial v} + P \right)\, \frac{\partial e}{\partial T}\bigg\rvert_p.\\ \end{aligned}\]

Q: If \(e = RT/2\) for a calorically perfect gas, then \(h = \alpha e\) where \(\alpha\) is \(\ldots\).

Q: Specific heat under constant pressure is defined as \(c_p = \frac{dq}{dT}\big\rvert_p\). Using the first law of thermodynamics and the basic definition of specific enthalpy, show that \(c_p = \frac{\partial h}{\partial T}\big\rvert_p\).

Objective questions

Q: Temperature is a path variable. - True

  • False

A: True

Q: A non-adiabatic process can be isentropic. - True

  • False

A: False

Q: Density is an intensive property. - True - False

A: True

Q: Specific volume is not an extensive property. - True - False

A: True

Q: An equilibrium state of a gas can be characterised by how many intensive properties? Note here that we are only interested in the intensive properties of the gas.

A: 2

Q: The specific internal energy of an isolated system is always constant. - True - False

A: True

Q: The SI unit of \(R\) (in \(pv = RT\)) is - J/(kilomole K) - J/(Kg K) - J/(kilomole C) - J/(Kg C)

A: J/(Kg K)

Q: Given that the ideal gas law for Hydrogen is expressed as \(pv = RT\) and also \(p\hat{v}=\hat{R}T\). Here \(\hat{v}\) is specifc volume of air per mole and \(v\) is the specific volume per mass. Then \({R}/{\hat{R}} =\)

A: 2

Q: Which thermodynamic variable can used to ascertain if two systems are in mechanical equilibrium? - Pressure - Temperature - Force

  • Specific Volume

A: Pressure

Q: Specific gas constant for air (being considered as a thermally perfect gas) is a function of temperature.

  • True

  • False

A: False

Q: Identify state variables in the following statement of the First law of thermodynamics \(de = dq - pdv\) - \(e\) - \(q\) - \(p\)

  • \(v\)

A: \(e\), \(p\), \(v\)

Q: An equilibrium state of a gas can be characterised by how many properties? Note here that we are interested in the intensive as well as extensive properties of the gas.

A: 3

Q: Write down the equation defining the total enthalpy of a system?

A: \(H = E + pV\)

Q: List the three types of equilibria that two systems have to be in order for them to be in thermodynamic equilibrium.

A: mechanical, thermodynamic and chemical equilibrium

Q: State true of false with one line explanation. Specific enthalpy of an isothermal system is constant.

A: False, because \(h = e + pv\) and \(pv = RT\) only for a calorically perfect gas.
