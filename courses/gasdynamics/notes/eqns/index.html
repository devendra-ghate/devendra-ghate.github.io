Conservation laws

Equation of state

\[\begin{aligned} \text{Perfect Gas}~ ~ ~ pv & = RT\\ \text{Vander Waal's gas}~ ~ ~ p &= \frac{RT}{v - b} - \frac{a}{v^2} \end{aligned}\] Note: Rest of the section holds for perfect gas.

Adiabatic reversible flow

\[ p v^{\gamma} = \text{const} \]

Adiabatic irreversible flow

\[\begin{aligned} \frac{p_2}{p_1} & = \frac{1 + \gamma M_1^2}{1 + \gamma M_2^2}~ ~&\text{(Momentum Conservation)}\\ \frac{T_2}{T_1} & = \frac{1 + (\gamma-1)/2 M_1^2}{1 + (\gamma-1)/2 M_2^2}~ ~ &\text{(Energy Conservation)}\\ \frac{ρ_2}{ρ_1} & = \left(\frac{p_2}{p_1}\right)\left(\frac{T_1}{T_2}\right) ~ ~ &\text{(State Equation of perfect gas)} \end{aligned}\]

Normal Shock

\[\begin{aligned} M_1^* M_2^* & = 1~ ~ \text{(Prandtl's Relation)}\\ \text{where,}~ ~ ~ ~M^* & = \sqrt{\frac{M^2(\gamma+1)/2 }{1 + M^2(\gamma-1)/2 }}\\ \end{aligned}\]

Please derive following equations using the previous relationships.

\[\begin{aligned} \text{It can be shown that}~ ~ M_2^2 & = \frac{1 + M_1^2(\gamma-1)/2 }{\gamma M_1^2 - (\gamma-1)/2}\\ \frac{p_2}{p_1} & = 1 + \frac{2\gamma}{\gamma+1}(M_1^2 -1)\\ \frac{\rho_2}{\rho_1} & = \frac{(\gamma+1)M_1^2}{(\gamma-1)M_1^2 + 2} \end{aligned}\]

Moving Normal Shock

Speed of a moving shock is given by

\[w_s = a_1 \left( \frac{\gamma+1}{2\gamma}\frac{p_2}{p_1} + \frac{\gamma-1}{2\gamma} \right)^{1/2}\]

It can be shown that

\[\lim_{p_2/p_1 \rightarrow \infty} w_s = \infty\]

Similarly,

\[\begin{aligned} \frac{\Delta s}{R} & = \frac{\gamma}{\gamma-1}\ln{\frac{T_2}{T_1}} - \ln{\frac{p_2}{p_1}} = \ln{\left(\frac{p_1}{p_2}\right) \left(\frac{T_2}{T_1}\right)^{(\gamma/(\gamma-1)}} = \ln{\frac{p_{0_1}}{p_{0_2}}}\\ \frac{\rho_2}{\rho_1} & = \frac{(\gamma+1)M_1^2 \sin^2{\beta}}{(\gamma-1)M_1^2 \sin^2{\beta} + 2} \\ \frac{p_2}{p_1} & = 1 + \frac{2\gamma}{\gamma+1}(M_1^2 \sin^2{\beta} -1) \end{aligned}\]

Oblique Shock

\[\tan{\theta} = \frac{2 \cot{\beta}(M_1^2 \sin^2{\beta} -1 )}{2 + M_1^2 (\gamma + \cos{2\beta})}\]
