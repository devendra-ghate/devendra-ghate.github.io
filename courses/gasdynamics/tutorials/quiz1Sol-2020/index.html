Quiz 1 (2020)

Q: The SI unit of enthalpy \(H\) is

A: Unit of enthalpy (\(H\)) is Joules. This is different from specific enthalpy (\(h\)) which has the unit of J/kg.

Q: Unit of mass-specific entropy is

A: J/(kg-K)

Q: Which thermodynamic property is used to ascertain if two systems are in thermodynamic equilibrium?

A: Clearly the answer is temperature. Pressure is used for mechanical equilibrium and mole fraction is used to ascertain chemical equilibrium.

Q: Identify path variables in the following statement of the first law of thermodynamics \(de = dq + dw\) - \(e\) - \(q\) - \(w\)

A: Varibles \(q\) and \(w\) are the path variables.

Q: Consider a flow between sections A and B of a variable area adiabatic duct. If the flow is in equilibrium at A and B, but is not in equilibrium in-between, then - \(T_{0_A} = T_{0_B}\) - \(T_{0_A} > T_{0_B}\) - \(P_{0_A} > P_{0_B}\) - \(P_{0_A} = P_{0_B}\)

A: Since the flow is not in equilibrium between sections A and B, therefore the flow is not isentropic. But mass, momentum and energy conservation hold. Hence, from energy conservation,

\[h_{0_A} = h_{0_B} ~~\Rightarrow~~T_{0_A} = T_{0_B}.\]

Also, we have shown that for non-equilibrium processes, entropy increases. Hence,

\[s_B - s_A = s_{0_B} - s_{0_A} > 0 ~~\Rightarrow~~p_{0_A} > p_{0_B}\]

Q: What is the minimum number of thermodynamic properties required to deduce all the other intensive as well as extensive properties of an equilibrium state of a system?

A: Two intensive properties of a system would define all other intensive properties. However, one more extensive property is required to fix the extensive properties also. Hence, in total minimum three properties are required. For example, pressure, temperature and mass.

Q: Kinetic theory of gases links the different types of kinetic energies of molecules to the thermodynamic quantity, internal energy (\(e\)). List all the types of molecular motions that contribute to the internal energy of a gas.

A: Internal energy of a gas is the sum of the translational, rotational and vibrational kinetic energies of the molecules.

Q: Air flows through a convergent-divergent nozzle. At station 1 (in the convergent part of the nozzle), \(u_1 = 150\text{m/s}\), \(T_1 = 315~\text{K}\) and \(p_1 = 152\text{kPa}\). What is the density at the throat if the flow at nozzle exit is supersonic. \(\gamma = 1.4\) and \(R = 287~\text{J/kg-K}\).

A: 

R = 287
M = u/sqrt(γ₁*R*T)
T₀ = T*( 1 + (γ₁-1)/2*M^2 )
p₀ = p * (T₀/T)^( γ₁/(γ₁ - 1) )
ρ₀ = p₀/R/T₀

γ = 1.4
# Inputs
u₁ = 150 # m/s
T₁ = 315 # K
p₁ = 152000 # pascal

# Calculations
M₁ = u₁/sqrt(γ*R*T₁)
ρ₁ = p₁/R/T₁

As the flow is supersonic at the exit, \(M_t = 1\). Hence, from the isentropic tables

\[A_1/A_t = 1.529\]

Now, the mass conservation equation

\[\begin{aligned} A_1 ρ_1 u_1 & = A_t ρ_t u_t\\ A_1 ρ_1 M_1 a_1 & = A_t ρ_t M_t a_t \\ \rho_t & = \rho_1 \left( \frac{A_1}{A_t}\right) \left( \frac{M_1 \sqrt{T_1}}{M_t \sqrt{T_t} }\right) \\ & = \rho_1 \left( \frac{A_1}{A_t}\right) \left( \frac{M_1 \sqrt{T_{0_t}/T_t}}{M_t \sqrt{T_{0_1}/T_1} }\right) \end{aligned}\]

which yields

\[\rho_t = ρ_1 M_1 \left( \frac{A_1}{A_t}\right) \sqrt{\frac{\frac{γ+1}{2}}{1 + \frac{γ-1}{2} M_1^2}}\]

This uses the isentropic relations for \(T_{0}/T\).

ρₜ = ρ₁*M₁*1.529*sqrt( ( (γ+1)/2 ) / (1 + ( (γ-1)/2 ) * M₁^2 ) )
